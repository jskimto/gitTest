Functional Safety

Functional Safety Certification

Our certification process focuses on just two key checkpoints — making it simpler and more affordable than you might expect.

The Two Points That Keep Costs Down

①
Random Hardware Failure

Using FMEDA (Failure Modes, Effects and Diagnostic Analysis), we quantitatively calculate the proportion of undetected dangerous failures that may occur at random in hardware.

②
Systematic Failure (Deterministic)

Verification of human-error prevention measures through manuals and procedures. This covers all phases: design, manufacturing, and software development.

Three Assessment Categories

Type 1 Product — Hardware Assessment

Random hardware failure assessment (FMEDA)

Type 2 Product — Software Assessment

Systematic failure avoidance assessment for software development (including V-model)

Type 3 Operator System Assessment

Comprehensive assessment covering safety requirements specification, hardware design/manufacturing/installation, software design, and human-error avoidance

Message from the Director

Greetings

The safety of workers on industrial sites takes precedence above all else. Since beginning operations in 2016, SEMA has served the industrial community as a functional safety certification body and an expert in the field of explosion protection.

In March 2026, we further expanded the scope of our explosion-proof consulting. We now provide a fully integrated service — from the design and certification of both electrical and non-electrical explosion-proof equipment, through to hazardous area zoning and post-commissioning maintenance.

"Stop being in awe of others." — As a functional safety certification body directly registered by the Japanese government, we stand on equal footing with European institutions and certify the safety of your products. Please feel free to reach out by email anytime.

SEMA's Policy

Information Protection Policy

SEMA implements comprehensive security measures to prevent unauthorized access, loss, damage, falsification, and leakage of information entrusted to us. All information will be responsibly disposed of one year after the completion of the relevant project (except for contact details and copies of deliverables).

Information provided will be used solely for communication from SEMA and for testing, evaluation, and certification work. It will not be disclosed to third parties without your consent. For details, please contact us.
